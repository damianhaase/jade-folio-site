Equinox Members Mobile App

At the end of year 2015, Business budgeted design and dev team to redesign the home screen and menu structure in order to continue satisfying the app users. The app rating was 4.5 star (App Store, 5 out of 5 as the highest), so keeping the happy customers happier was a challenge while satisfying some business needs. We identified the detailed issues, limitations or areas for improvement based on our deep understanding of member behaviors and lifestyle. Below, some of the design opportunities were identified as researching the projects.

App Background

In 2015 Q3, Equinox had 273k members. Out of them, there were 214k online account registered users, 68k website users and 127k app users (only iOS supported) on any given month. On average month, usage for the app is such as below (example October 2015):

  • Have class on calendar: 56K
  • Booked class 37K
  • Updated weight: 38K
  • Joined last challenge: 5500
  • Referrals sent: 3200
  • Logged custom workout: 4800
  • Logged OCR workout: 1800
  • Booked spa: 1000
  • Buy gift card: 800
  • Requested Equifit: 500

Current Limitations

  • Navigation does not scale as we add new features/sections
  • Navigation is inconsistent and unbalanced
  • Home screen is merely task-oriented/navigational
  • Home screen is not personalized
  • Home screen is not motivating
  • Home screen is not aesthetically pleasing
  • Marketing/promotional space is limited
  • Although most members are on the app, many revenue-generating features are not available there

Goals

The goal for the new design was to keep their core functionality of task based actions (about 85% of current traffic) as the major, while bringing the other performance review and discovery aspect higher.

Ideas

Many ideas were drawn, reviewed, and drawn more.

Testing

Testing goals, tasks, objectives are still in discussion. Scouting was through database team, based on my target audience specifics.

Prototype was built and design exploration started by visual designers.

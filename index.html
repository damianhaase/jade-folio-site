I have been practicing user experience design since 2008 in software products, applications, websites and interior spaces.

In my late teens, I wanted to be a product designer, who makes nice things. At the time, Target had product designer collaborations with their household items, such as Kaarim Rashid and Dirt Devil or Philippe Starck and tape dispenser. It was eye opening that your vacuum cleaner could look so different and attractive. I became obsessed with well-made modern product designs such as by Dieter Rams, Don Norman and of course, Apple. At the end of my college year, I had an internship with Philips where I started my first career as an interaction designer. My design domain has changed, so my techniques have changed over time, but my definition of good design remains more or less the same. Strong intent, methodical process and attention to details - this is my success formula.

I live in Brooklyn, NY. My favorite fruit is banana and color is yellow, and not sure what else is appropriate to talk about. So please ask me!
